## Indian Entrepreneur Success Stories

- Sachin and Binny Bansal launched Flipkart, pioneering Indian e-commerce, and eventually sold to Walmart in a record deal.
    
- Bhavish Aggarwal started Ola Cabs, transforming public transportation in Indian cities.
    
- Deepinder Goyal (Zomato) and Ritesh Agarwal (OYO Rooms) built tech-driven brands that addressed local needs and expanded globally.
    
- Nandan Nilekani (Infosys) and Falguni Nayar (Nykaa) exemplify how Indian entrepreneurs have created global market leaders from scratch.

## Businesses and Sectors Transformed

- Flipkart, Ola, Zomato, and OYO have changed retail, transport, food delivery, and hospitality.
    
- Companies like Biocon and B9 Beverages (Bira 91) have disrupted biosciences and the beverage space.
    
- Social enterprises such as SELCO (sustainable energy), Frontier Markets (rural women entrepreneurship), and Robin Hood Army (food for the needy) demonstrate broad social impact.

## Government Policies & Schemes

- Startup India: Offers tax breaks, quick company registration, easier compliance, IPR support, and seed funding.
    
- Make in India: Provides incentives for local manufacturing, sectoral FDI reforms, and Production Linked Incentives (PLIs) to boost exports and indigenous production.
    
- PMEGP: Helps micro and small entrepreneurs with capital subsidies and loans.
    
- Stand-Up India: Earmarks funding support especially for women and SC/ST entrepreneurs.
    
- Mudra Yojana: Facilitates micro-loans for early-stage and rural businesses.
    
- Udyogini, Mahila Coir Yojana, and other women-centric schemes support skilling, market linkage, and financial access for women.

## Social and Grassroots Entrepreneurship

- Harish Hande’s SELCO delivers solar energy to rural households.
    
- Aarushi Batra’s Robin Hood Army tackles hunger by redistributing food surplus.
    
- Ajaita Shah’s Frontier Markets promotes rural women as last-mile energy and product entrepreneurs.
    
- Jeroo Billimoria’s Childline supports vulnerable children at the grassroots level.
    

---

## Policy Impact and Trends

- Startup India and Make in India have accelerated company registration and access to venture funding.
    
- MSME and women-focused policies have enabled more first-time and grassroots entrepreneurs.
    
- Cities like Bengaluru, Delhi, and Mumbai have become startup magnets, but Tier-II/Tier-III cities are also rapidly catching up due to digitalization and policy reach.
