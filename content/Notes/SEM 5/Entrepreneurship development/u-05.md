## Support to Entrepreneurs by Agencies

## 1. District Industries Centres (DIC)

- **Role:** Serve as one-stop support centers for MSMEs at the district level by providing guidance, training, business planning, and assistance in obtaining licenses and loans.
    
- **Supports Entrepreneurs by:**
    
    1. Offering seed money assistance (usually 15-20% of project cost).
        
    2. Facilitating bank loans (covers about 75% of project cost).
        
    3. Providing entrepreneurship development training via approved NGOs.
        
    4. Offering registration and certification for MSMEs.
        
    5. Skill development and marketing assistance.
        
- **Example:** DIC helps rural entrepreneurs access MUDRA loans and government subsidies to start small businesses.[](https://doingbusinessinmaharashtra.org/DIC_schemes.php)​
    

## 2. Small Industries Development Bank of India (SIDBI)

- **Role:** Provides financial support, refinancing, and developmental assistance targeting MSMEs to promote growth and modernization.
    
- **Supports Entrepreneurs by:**
    
    1. Offering indirect financing/refinancing to banks for MSME loans.
        
    2. Providing direct financing for service sectors, risk capital, and sustainable growth.
        
    3. Implementing various schemes like technology development and equity funds.
        
- **Example:** SIDBI finances MSMEs to upgrade technology and expand production capacity.[](https://byjus.com/free-ias-prep/small-industries-development-bank-of-india-sidbi/)​
    

## 3. State Industrial Development Corporations (SIDCO)

- **Role:** Facilitate industrial estate development, infrastructure, raw material supply, and consultancy to small scale enterprises.
    
- **Supports Entrepreneurs by:**
    
    1. Providing land and infrastructure for small manufacturing units.
        
    2. Offering marketing and technical support.
        
    3. Advising on project commissioning and raw material procurement.
        
- **Example:** Kerala SIDCO facilitates site identification and project commissioning for small industries.[](https://vidyaprasar.dei.ac.in/wp-content/uploads/2022/03/Lesson-12.pdf)​
    

## 4. Small Scale Industries Board (SSIB)

- **Role:** Coordinate policy, advocacy, and support for small-scale industries.
    
- **Supports Entrepreneurs by:**
    
    1. Providing financial assistance through subsidies and grants.
        
    2. Offering technical support and skill development programs.
        
    3. Facilitating counseling and guidance on project feasibility and business planning.
        
- **Example:** SSIB runs skill upgradation programs to boost SSI competitiveness.[](https://theintactone.com/2024/05/18/small-scale-industries-board-role/)​
    

## 5. National Small Industries Corporation (NSIC)

- **Role:** Provides integrated marketing, technology, finance, and development support services to MSMEs.
    
- **Supports Entrepreneurs by:**
    
    1. Organizing trade fairs and marketing linkages.
        
    2. Facilitating raw material procurement at competitive prices.
        
    3. Providing technical training and quality testing services.
        
    4. Offering financial schemes such as credit guarantees.
        
- **Example:** NSIC supports MSMEs in gaining access to global markets and technology.[](https://www.nsic.co.in/Corporate/AboutUs)​
    

## 6. Small Industries Service Institutes (SISI)

- **Role:** Provide technical and managerial counseling and conduct entrepreneurship programs.
    
- **Supports Entrepreneurs by:**
    
    1. Advising on machinery selection, quality control, and energy conservation.
        
    2. Organizing training workshops and seminars on emerging business topics.
        
    3. Disseminating market and policy information.
        
- **Example:** SISI trains entrepreneurs on pollution control and financial management.[](https://dspmuranchi.ac.in/pdf/Blog/ilide.info-promoting-small-business-development-the-roles-of-key-government-agencies-in-in-pr_0d9903fa9a0b6ac1cb645d08c87404a4.pdf)​
    

## Entrepreneurship Promotion by Government through Various Schemes

- **Startup India:** Mentoring, funding through 'Fund of Funds', incubation, ease of compliance, and a free online learning program.
    
- **Make in India:** Encourages manufacturing growth with funding and infrastructure support.
    
- **Atal Innovation Mission (AIM):** Promotes innovation and entrepreneurship.
    
- **Stand-Up India:** Provides bank loans to women, SC, and ST entrepreneurs for starting enterprises.
    
- **PMEGP (Prime Minister’s Employment Generation Program):** Offers financial assistance to MSMEs.
    
- **TREAD:** Supports women's entrepreneurship with training and credit access.
    
- **National Skill Development Mission:** Enhances entrepreneurial skills across sectors.
    

These schemes offer financial aid, training, credit facilities, technical support, and marketing opportunities to enable new and existing entrepreneurs to establish and grow their ventures.
