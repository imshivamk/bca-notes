## MINERAL RESOURCES

1. <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">Naturally occurring elements or compounds that have been formed through slow inorganic processes.</mark>
2. <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">Modern civilization is based on the use and exploitation of mineral resources. </mark>Minerals can be metallic and non-metallic.
3. **not evenly distributed in the Earth.** Some countries are rich in mineral deposits whereas others are devoid of it.
4. **Important for development** worldwide. 
5. With rapid increase in population and a more rapid increase in society’s development needs, the requirements for minerals have grown and diversified manifold.
6. **Extraction of minerals** is carried out through mining. Minerals are extracted from beneath the surface, processed, and used for different purposes.
7. **exhaustible and finite**, which means **excessive use affects their availability** in the future.

## EXPLOITATION OF MINERALS

<mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">Use of mineral resources for economic growth</mark>. Exploitation of mineral resources at a <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">mindless speed to meet the growing needs of modern civilization has resulted in many environmental problems.</mark>

1. Although, the **exploitation of minerals began at a slow pace during the industrial revolution** in Western countries, **during the 20th century, the exploitation of some minerals, especially the fossil fuels increased exponentially** to meet the growing energy need. 
2. Today, about **80% of the world’s energy consumption is sustained by the extraction of fossil fuels, which consists of oil, coal, and gas.**
