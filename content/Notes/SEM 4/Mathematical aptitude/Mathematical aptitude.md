## **1. Problems on Numbers**

- **Divisibility Tests**: Rules to check if a number is divisible by 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 9, 11, etc.
- **Basic Arithmetic Operations**: Addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.

![](https://i.imgur.com/gYNusU9.png)

**Example**:

- Is **462** divisible by **11**?
    - Sum of digits at odd places = 4 + 2 = 6
    - Sum of digits at even places = 6
    - Difference = 6 - 6 = 0 (Divisible by 11 ✅)

## **2. LCM (Least Common Multiple) & HCF (Highest Common Factor)**

- **LCM**: The smallest number divisible by given numbers.
- **HCF**: The largest number that divides given numbers.

![](https://i.imgur.com/9USToS0.png)


**Example**:  
Find **LCM and HCF** of **12 and 18**.

- Prime factorization:
    - 12 = **2² × 3**
    - 18 = **2 × 3²**
- **LCM = 2² × 3² = 36**
- **HCF = 2 × 3 = 6**

## **3. Profit and Loss**

- **Profit (%)** = (Profit / Cost Price) × 100
- **Loss (%)** = (Loss / Cost Price) × 100

**Example**:  
A product is bought for ₹200 and sold for ₹250. Find the profit %.

- **Profit** = 250 - 200 = ₹50
- **Profit %** = (50/200) × 100 = **25%**

---

## **4. Partnership**

- If **A invests x** and **B invests y**, profits are divided in **x : y** ratio.
- If invested for different time periods, use **x × time : y × time**.

**Example**:

- A invests ₹5000 for **6 months**.
- B invests ₹7000 for **8 months**.
- Profit Ratio = (5000 × 6) : (7000 × 8) = **30:56**

## **5. Speed, Distance & Time**

- **Speed = Distance / Time**
- **Time = Distance / Speed**
- **Distance = Speed × Time**
- **Conversions**:
    - 1 km/hr = **5/18** m/s
    - 1 m/s = **18/5** km/hr

**Example**:  
A train moves at **72 km/hr**. Convert to **m/s**.

- (72 × 5) / 18 = **20 m/s**

---

## **6. Simple & Compound Interest**

- **Simple Interest (SI)** = (P × R × T) / 100
- **Compound Interest (CI)** = P(1 + R/100)^T - P
- **Compound Interest (CI)** = A - P

![](https://i.imgur.com/7rNrqIb.png)


**Example**:  
Find **SI** for ₹1000 at 10% per annum for 2 years.

- SI = (1000 × 10 × 2) / 100 = **₹200**

## **7. Problems on Ages**

- Use equations based on given conditions.

**Example**:  
Father is **twice** his son's age. After **5 years**, their total age is **65**. Find current ages.

- Let son’s age = **x**
- Father’s age = **2x**
- (x + 5) + (2x + 5) = 65
- 3x + 10 = 65 → **x = 18**
- Father’s age = **36**

## **8. Simplification**

- **Follow BODMAS** (Brackets, Orders, Division, Multiplication, Addition, Subtraction).

**Example**:  
Solve **(10 + 2 × 3) ÷ 2**

- **= (10 + 6) ÷ 2**
- **= 16 ÷ 2**
- **= 8**
