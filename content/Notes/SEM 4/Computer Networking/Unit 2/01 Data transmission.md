## Data Transmission

1. Data transmission refers to the process of sending and receiving digital or analog data between two or more devices over a communication medium.
## Types of Data Transmission

#### 1. Simplex Transmission

1. Data flows only in one direction.
2. No two-way communication is possible.
3. Example: Television broadcasting, Radio transmission.

#### 2. Half-Duplex Transmission

1. - Data flows in both directions, but only one at a time.
2. - Devices take turns in sending and receiving data.
3. - Example: Walkie-talkies, CB radios.

#### 3. Full-Duplex Transmission

1. Data flows in both directions simultaneously.
2. Increases efficiency compared to half-duplex.
3. Example: Telephone communication, Video calls.

#### Serial Transmission

1. Data is sent bit by bit (one at a time) in sequence over a single communication channel.
2. Slower but suitable for long-distance communication.
3. Example: USB, RS-232 communication, Fiber optics.

#### Parallel transmission

1. Multiple bits are transmitted simultaneously using multiple channels.
2.  Faster but limited to short distances due to signal interference.
3. Example: Internal CPU communication, Older printer cables
