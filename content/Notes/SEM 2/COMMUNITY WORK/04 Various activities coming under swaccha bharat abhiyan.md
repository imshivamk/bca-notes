### 1. Construction of Toilets:
   - Building individual household toilets to eliminate open defecation.
   - Construction of community and public toilets in urban and rural areas.

### 2. Promotion of Sanitary Practices:
   - Conducting awareness campaigns to educate people about the importance of using toilets and adopting good hygiene practices.
   - Promoting the use of sanitary napkins and menstrual hygiene management among women.

### 3. Waste Management:
   - Implementation of waste segregation at source to separate biodegradable and non-biodegradable waste.
   - Encouraging recycling and reuse of materials.
   - Establishing waste treatment and disposal facilities.

### 4. Cleanliness Drives:
   - Organizing cleanliness drives at the community level, involving citizens, local authorities, and volunteers.
   - Regular cleaning and maintenance of public spaces, including streets, parks, and markets.

### 5. Swachh Bharat Survekshan:
   - Conducting cleanliness surveys to assess and rank cities based on their sanitation and cleanliness practices.
   - Encouraging healthy competition among cities to improve their cleanliness rankings.

### 6. School Sanitation Programs:
   - Promoting cleanliness and hygiene in schools by providing proper sanitation facilities.
   - Conducting awareness programs on sanitation and hygiene among school children.

### 7. Public Awareness Campaigns:
   - Launching nationwide campaigns through various media channels to raise awareness about cleanliness and sanitation.
   - Involving celebrities and influencers to amplify the message.

### 8. Open Defecation-Free (ODF) Campaign:
   - Working towards achieving Open Defecation-Free status for villages and cities.
   - Encouraging communities to build and use toilets, thus eliminating open defecation.

### 9. Swachh Bharat Kosh:
   - Establishing funds for corporate social responsibility (CSR) initiatives to support cleanliness and sanitation projects.
   - Encouraging public and private sector participation in funding Swachh Bharat initiatives.

### 10. Community Mobilization:
- Involving local communities in planning and implementing sanitation projects.
- Empowering community leaders and volunteers to take an active role in promoting cleanliness.

### 11. Technology Integration:
- Using technology, such as mobile apps, to monitor and track the progress of sanitation initiatives.
- Implementing digital solutions for better waste management and public awareness.

### 12. Rural Sanitation Programs:
- Focusing on sanitation and hygiene in rural areas through the construction of toilets and awareness campaigns.
- Promoting the use of innovative and cost-effective technologies for rural sanitation.

These activities collectively aim to create a sustainable and comprehensive approach to achieving the goals of the Swachh Bharat Abhiyan, contributing to a cleaner and healthier India.
