## What are natural resources?

1. Resources <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">obtained from nature, i.e. the earth</mark> are called natural resources
2. occur naturally, and humans cannot make them.
3. The <mark style="background: #FFB8EBA6;">raw materials</mark> used in artificial or man-made resources are natural resources.
## SCOPE -
1. RENEWABLE
2. NON RENNEWABLE

## Classification of natural resources

1. On the basis of their origin
	1. living or biotic resources
	2. non-living or abiotic resources.
2. On the basis of deposit or stock
	1. renewable
	2. non-renewable.

### 1. BIOTIC / LIVING NATURAL RESOURCES

1. If natural resources <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">come from living things or organic materials</mark> they are termed as living or biotic resources.
2. Biotic resources include <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">plants, animals and fossil fuels</mark>.
3. Fossil fuels such as coal, oil and natural gas are classified as biotic resources as they are <mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">formed from the decay of organic matter over millions of years.</mark>

### 2. ABIOTIC / NON-LIVING NATURAL RESOURCES

1. if the resources are <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">derived from nonliving or inorganic materials,</mark> they are termed as abiotic resources.
2. For instance,<mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;"> air, sunlight, and water</mark> are abiotic natural resources.
3. <mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">Minerals</mark> are also considered abiotic.

### 1. RENEWABLE NATURAL RESOURCES

1. Resources that <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">can be used without any risk of its ending up</mark> are called renewable resources.
2. They exist in unlimited quantity.
3. Get replenished quickly once depleted
4.<mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;"> Sun, water, wind, biomass, tides, geothermal energy</mark>, etc. are renewable resources.
5. These are infinite sources of energy.

### 2. NON-RENEWABLE NATURAL RESOURCES

1. <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">cannot be replenished after their depletion</mark> is called non-renewable resources.
2. Most <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">fossil fuels, such as coal, petroleum and natural</mark> gas are considered nonrenewable resources.
3. Nonrenewable resources <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">take millions of years for their formation</mark>, 
4. hence, their<mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;"> cautious and economic use</mark> is the only option left for mankind.
