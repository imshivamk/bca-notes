## What is GAAP

1. <mark style="background: #FFB8EBA6;">A set of common accounting rules,  standards, and procedure</mark> issued by the <mark style="background: #FFB8EBA6;">financial accounting standard board (FASB)</mark>
2. combination of<mark style="background: #FFB8EBA6;"> AUTHORITIVE standards</mark> set by the policy board
2. the commonly accepted ways of recording and reporting financial info
3. aims to improve <mark style="background: #ABF7F7A6;">CLARITY, CONSISTENCY AND COMPARABILITY</mark>  of communication of info
4. Public companies MUST follow GAAP
5. Ultimate goal of GAAP is to ensure a companies financial statements and COMPLETE and CONSISTENT

## Principles of GAAP

#### 1. Regularity
1. its a given that he accountant has <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">adhered to the GAAP rules and regulations as a standard</mark>
2. <mark style="background: #ABF7F7A6;">all the rules and policies</mark> of a standard accounting practice are followed in the organization on a regular basis
#### 2. Consistency
1. accountant should <mark style="background: #FFB8EBA6;">apply the same rules and standards throughout the whole reporting process over one financial period.</mark>
2. It <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">ensures financial comparison</mark> between two periods
3. <mark style="background: #FFB8EBA6;">Fully disclose any change or update in accounting standards</mark>
#### 3. Sincerity
1. accountant should provide an<mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;"> accurate and impartial picture of the companies financial situation</mark>
#### 4. Permanence of methods (consistent and comparable)
1. The <mark style="background: #FFB8EBA6;">procedure</mark> used in fin. accounting should be <mark style="background: #FFB8EBA6;">consistent and comparable</mark> to the companies financial information
#### 5. Non-Compensation (transparent and Impartial)
1. Accountant must<mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;"> record both POSITIVE and NEGATIVES in financial statement</mark>
2. Both should be reported with<mark style="background: #FFB8EBA6;"> full transparency</mark>
#### 6. Continuity (perpetuity)
1. While valuing assets, it should be assumed that the <mark style="background: #CACFD9A6;">business will continue to operate in the future</mark>
#### 7. Periodicity 
1. All the accounting <mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">entries should be distributed across the appropriate periods of time.</mark>
2. e. g <mark style="background: #FFB8EBA6;">Revenue should be reported on its relevant accounting-period</mark>
#### 8. Materiality (disclosure)
1. Accountant <mark style="background: #FFB8EBA6;">must fully disclose of all financial data and accounting information in financial reports</mark>
#### 9. Good Faith (honesty)
1. It pre-supposes that all the <mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">parties involved remain honest in all transactions</mark>
2. The word is commonly used in <mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">Insurance industry</mark>
