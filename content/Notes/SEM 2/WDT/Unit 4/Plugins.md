**What is a WordPress Plugin?**

1. A WordPress plugin is a piece of<mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;"> software that adds specific features or functionality to your WordPress website.</mark>
2. These plugins are <mark style="background: #FFF3A3A6;">designed to extend and enhance the capabilities of your site without altering the core WordPress software</mark>.
3. Plugins can offer a wide range of features, such as <mark style="background: #ABF7F7A6;">SEO optimization</mark>, <mark style="background: #D2B3FFA6;">security enhancements, contact forms, e-commerce functionality, and more.</mark>

**Steps to Install, Activate, Deactivate, and Delete a WordPress Plugin:**

**1. Log in to your WordPress Dashboard:**
   - Access your WordPress admin panel by going to `http://yourdomain.com/wp-admin` and log in.

**2. Navigate to the Plugins Section:**
   - Go to `Plugins > Installed Plugins` in the WordPress admin menu.

### Installing a WordPress Plugin:

**3. Click on "Add New":**
   - On the Installed Plugins page, click on the "Add New" button.

**4. Search or Upload:**
   - Use the search bar to find a plugin by name or browse through featured, popular, or recommended plugins.
   - If you have a plugin in a .zip file, click on "Upload Plugin" and choose the file from your computer.

**5. Install and Activate:**
   - After finding or uploading a plugin, click the "Install Now" button.
   - Once installed, click the "Activate" button to activate the plugin.

### Deactivating a WordPress Plugin:

**6. Deactivate a Plugin:**
   - To deactivate a plugin, go to `Plugins > Installed Plugins`.
   - Find the plugin you want to deactivate and click the "Deactivate" link below its name.

### Deleting a WordPress Plugin:

**7. Delete a Plugin:**
   - To delete a plugin, go to `Plugins > Installed Plugins`.
   - Deactivate the plugin if it's currently active.
   - Once deactivated, you'll see a "Delete" link below the plugin's name. Click on it.
   - Confirm the deletion when prompted.
