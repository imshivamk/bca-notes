1. Database models show the <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">logical structure of a database</mark> and the <mark style="background: #BBFABBA6;">relationships and constraints</mark> that<mark style="background: #ABF7F7A6;"> determine how data can be accessed and stored.</mark>
2.  <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">the way the information is stored and retrieved from a database. </mark>
3. Defines the <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">rules</mark> by which the database will operate.
4. <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;"> integrated collection of concepts describing and manipulating data</mark>, as well as <mark style="background: #ABF7F7A6;">constraints on the data within an organization.</mark>
5. It is a <mark style="background: #ADCCFFA6;">conceptual representation of physical data stored</mark> in the database.
6. Data models define <mark style="background: #FFB8EBA6;">how data is connected to each other</mark> and <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">how it's processed and stored inside the system.</mark>
7. gives an idea of <mark style="background: #FFB8EBA6;">how the final system would look after implementation.</mark>
8. It specifies the data items as well as the relationships between them.
9. In a database management system, data models are often used to show how data is connected, stored, accessed, and changed.
10. Some of the Data Models in DBMS are:
	1. <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">Hierarchical</mark> Model
	2. <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">Network</mark> Model
	3. <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">Entity-Relationship</mark> Model
	4. <mark style="background: #FFB86CA6;">Relational</mark> Model
